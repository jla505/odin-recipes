Ramen

Description

Ramen is a Japanese noodle dish. It consists of Chinese-style wheat noodles served in a broth; common flavors are soy sauce and miso, with typical toppings including sliced pork, nori, menma, and scallions. Ramen has its roots in Chinese noodle dishes.

Ingredients

  • 4 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tsp of ginger, grated
  • 2 tbsp of soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp of miso paste
  • 2 packs of ramen noodles
  • 1 cup of sliced shiitake mushrooms
  • 2 soft boiled eggs, peeled and halved
  • 2 stalks of green onions, chopped
  • 1/2 cup of sliced bamboo shoots
  • 1/4 cup of nori seaweed, shredded
  • 1 tsp of sesame oil

Steps

  1. In a large pot, bring the broth to a simmer. Add the garlic, ginger, soy sauce, and miso paste. Stir until the miso paste is dissolved.
  2. Add the sliced shiitake mushrooms and bamboo shoots to the pot. Let simmer for 10-15 minutes until the mushrooms are soft.
  3. Cook the ramen noodles according to package instructions. Drain and rinse under cold water.
  4. Add the cooked noodles to the pot with the broth and vegetables. Stir until the noodles are coated in the broth.
  5. Divide the noodles and broth between two bowls. Top each bowl with a soft boiled egg, sliced green onions, and shredded nori seaweed.
  6. Drizzle each bowl with sesame oil before serving. Enjoy!
